Pure Vita Labs

THE CANADIAN WHEY

Brand FilmCinematicMulti-Athlete

The pilot.

When Pure Vita Labs prepared to launch CanWhey, their first fully Canadian-made protein, they didn't want a product ad. They wanted a statement. We built one around a single idea: what actually makes a Canadian athlete. Not the medals or the headlines, but the grit, the adaptability, the respect, and the quiet work that defines how we compete. We turned that into "The Canadian Whey," a cinematic hero commercial narrated like a national address, where six elite Canadian athletes each embody one piece of that identity.

The roster was built to span the Canadian sports landscape, not just one sport. Jalen Philpot (WR, Calgary Stampeders) carried earned talent. Dawn Richardson-Wilson (Olympic bobsledder, 3x U Sports 60m champion) carried adaptability, moving between the ice and the track. Taylor Makar and Christiano Aiello brought grit and respect through a staged hockey fight that ends in a handshake. Nate Petrone (U Sports MVP) embodied legacy, both on the court and coaching kids in the community. Nick Pelletier rounded it out with relentlessness in the water. Every athlete was framed against the same recurring visual language so five separate shoots, across 7 distinct locations, cut together as one unified film.

We built a full campaign around the drop: a teaser video and a series of media pieces released ahead of launch to create anticipation, complemented by the featured athletes posting their own content promoting the product across their personal pages. The film carried the story, and the surrounding rollout turned a single release into a moment.

This was Colosseum's first project, and we treated it like a proving ground. We wrote the full narration script before a single frame was shot, built every athlete's segment around a specific theme, and ran five distinct shoots, each with its own location and creative plan engineered to intercut seamlessly. We also scripted and recorded the voiceover that carries the entire film, written to match the message and delivered like a national address, so the words and the footage hit as one. The result set the standard for how we build everything now: story first, locked creative before the camera comes out, and execution built to carry a message, not just fill a screen.
